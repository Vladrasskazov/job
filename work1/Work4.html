Glossary

3D-Planning

3D-Planning makes the of operation procedures for implants more predictable and safer. Especially in difficult cases with little remaining bone structure, computer tomography is used to facilitate and prepare the preoperative planning.

Amalgam

Amalgam is a dental restorative alloy containing mercury and other materials. Alternatives are synthetic material, ceramic and gold restaurations

Anaesthesia

Anaesthesia is the term for temporarily blocking or taking away certain physical functions in order to make diagnostical or surgical interventions tolerable.

Bleaching

Bleaching whitens the teeth by applying hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide (external bleaching) or filling it into a canal treated tooth (internal bleaching). The interfering colouring agents are decomposed and the teeth are brightened. The bleaching effects lasts for about one to three years.

Bleeding gums

Bleeding gums result from an inflammatory disease of the gum.

Bone reconstruction

Dental bone reconstruction are measures to fill weak parts of the jaw with well tolerated materials. The organism is stimulated to build new bone structures that grows into the artificial material and progressively replaces it. Thus, missing bone can be restructured.

Botulinum toxin

(Popular name: Botox) A therapeutic agent used for wrinkle injections or for plastic surgery in cases of deformied or destroyed the teeth or the jaw. Dentists inject these neurotoxic proteins to fill, correct and restore the mouth.

Carbamide peroxide

Carbamide peroxide is mainly used as a bleaching or disinfecting agent in consumer goods such as bleaching, straighening and perm formulars, ear drops and antiseptic mouthwash, in products to relieve inflammation of gums, toothpaste and toothbleach. It is also used in contact lens fluids and for desinfection.

Caries

Caries is a progressive destruction of the tooth structure caused by the bacterial acids. Untreat careis can have severe consequences, eg. Painful inflammations of the dental roots or the „famous“ cracks and holes in the tooth.

Ceramic

Currently ceramic is the material meeting the highest aesthetic requirements and on top it is well tolerated. Metal-reinforced restorations can cause intolerances, allergies, mucosal discolourations or taste disturbance.

CMD

Craniomandibular dysfunction (CMD) is a disorder of the masticatory system, the jaw joint, the jaw muscles or the bite.

Curettage

The open (flap operation) or closed curettage are periodontal treatments.

Dental bridge

A dental bridge is a fixed partial denture used to replace a missing tooth. A dental bridge is fixed by joining it permanently to the two adjacent teeth.

Dental floss

Dental floss helps to clean the spaces between the teeth from plaque and food particles. Dentists recommend the daily use of dental floss supporting the regular tooth cleaning. Using dental floss prevents caries and also periodotitis.

Dental plaque

Plaque consists of several complex layers and contains proteins, carbihydrates, phospates and microorganisms. Plaque develops where the tooth surface can’tbe kept plaque free by natural or artificial cleaning.

Dental pulp

The dental pul fills the cavity in the tooth crown and the tooth root. It consists of tissue, blood vessels, nerves and lymphatics. Bundles of nerves and tissue reach from the pulp to the dentine. The nerve fibres of the pulp react to any irritation with a pain sensation.

Denture

Denture is the generic term for implants, dental crowns, bridges, partial or full dentures.

DVT

Digital volume tomography (DVT) is an X-ray based 3D imaging tomography method.

Early Diagnosis

An early diagnosis of tooth diseases is the most important factor for a succesful treatment. This makes regular examination of the tooth so important

Endodontics

Endodontics is a root canal therapy applied for cleaning and filling the root canal system.

Flap operation

In contrast to closed curettage, during the flap operation the gum is cut from the tooth and the bone in order to clean the root surface. After monitoring the depth of the gingival pockets, the operation is carried out under local anaesthesia.

Fluoridation

Using fluorinated laquers or jelly containing agents in order to harden the tooth substance. Fluorine supports the tooth’s absorbation of minerals in order to lower the risk of caries.

Full dentures

Full dentures replace the complete set of teeth if the patient has lost all teeth. Full dentures consist of a basis customized to the remaining gum. Fixed to this basis are the artificial teeth. A thin film of saliva fixes the full denture to the mucosa, similar to a film of water between two sheets of glass.

Gingival pocket

The space between the gum and the tooth. Only a very thin slit in a healthy tooth bed, it can become very wide as a result of peroidontitis. A treatment of the tooth bed then becomes inevitable in order tp prevent the loss of the tooth.

Gingivitis

Gingivitis is an inflammation of the gum tissue, often resulting from plaque. The symptoms of gingivitis are swollen, bright red or purple gums that are tender or painful to the touch.

Gutta-percha

Gutta-Percha is an inelastic natural latex produced from the sap of tropical Gutta-percha native to Southeast Asia and northern Australasia. In dentistry it is mainly used for root canal treatments and somtimes for temporary fillings, e.g. in order to bridge the time between preparing and inserting an inlay. In contrast to synthetical temporary materials, fillings made of gutta-percha can be removed in one piece.

Halitosis

Halitosis originates from volatile sulfur compounds that mix with the exhaled air. These substances are produced e.g. during the bacterial decomposition of food residue or dead tissue.

Hyaluronic Acid

Hyaluronic acid is used, amongst others, for wrinkle treatment, for modelling lips, freshening the skin or to shape the facial contours. Depending on the stabilisation of the hyaluronic acid the effect lasts for 6 – 12 months. Agents with different size of the gel particles are available for various treatments.

Hydrogen peroxide

Hydrogen peroxide is mainly used as a bleaching or disinfecting agent in consumer goods such as bleaching, straighening and perm formulars, ear drops and antiseptic mouthwash, in products to relieve inflammation of gums, toothpaste and toothbleach. It is also used in contact lens fluids and for desinfection.

Immune System

The immune system is the protection system of biological structures and processes. Portections systems can be weak or strong – the immune system protects the organism against pathogenic agents and diseases. In case of a comparatively weak immune system, one will fall ill quicker and be more prone to illesses. The immune system is genetically determined and it can be influenced. All living beings have all immune system functions.

Implants

An implant is a artificial device manufactured to replace a missing biological structure, support a damaged biological structure or enhance existing biological structure or functions.

Jaw

The jaw is the bone strutcure embedding the teeth. The upper jaw is fixed to the skull, the jaw joint keeps the lower jaw moveable.

Laser therapy

One of the advantages of laser therapy is that stitching the wound becomes unnecessary and bleedings can virtually be ruled out. The laser sterilizes the treatment points so that bacterial infections occur very rarely. Laser therapy also results in shorter healing times compared to conventional methods.

Lower jaw

The lower jaw, also called ‘mandible’, is a bone of the facial skull. Of the two jawvbones, the lower jaw is the moveable one.

Mastication

Mastication is a mechanical process that is very important for the digestion as it crushes and chops the food. The mastication process is an up-and-down- movement of the lower jaw, combined with a lateral movement, mixing the food and saliva.

Ozone therapy

Certain methods in the field of alternative medicine applying ozone gas are called ozone therapy. Some dentists use ozone in order to treat caries without drilling. Ozone is also used for root canal desinfection or sterilisation of the tooth surface.

OPG

An orthopantomogram (OPG or panorex) is a panoramic scanning dental X-ray of the upper and lower jaw. It shows a two-dimensional view of a half-circle from ear to ear: all teeth, the jaws, the jaw joints as well as the right and the left maxilary sinus are depicted. An OPG also shows the lateral neck making arteriosclerosis of the carotid visible, the only annoying factor being the x-ray shadow of the spine.

Orthopaedics

Orthopedic surgery or orthopaedics (also spelled orthopedics) is the branch of surgery concerned with the development, prevention, diagnostics and treatment of congenital or acquired conditions involving the musculoskeletal system i.e. the bones, the joints, muscles, tendon – and the patients’ rehabilitation.

Palate

The palate is the upper wall ort he ceiling of the oral cavity.

Partial dentures

Partial dentures are mostl made of ceramic fixed to the remaining healthy teeth. They rebuild the chewing function and improve blurred articulation caused by missing teeth. On top they ensure a good cosmetic result.

Periodontitis

Periodontitis is a set of bacterial inflammatory diseases affecting and irreversibly destructing the tissues that surround and support the teeth.

Prophylaxis

Prophylaxis is any medical procedure whose purpose is to prevent a disease, i.e. precaution.

Prosthetics

Dental prosthetics or prosthetic dentistry, is a dental specialty pertaining to the rehabilitation of missing teeth and parts of the jaws. It includes all relevant biological, functional, psychosocial, material related and technological aspects.

PZR

Professional cleaning is a prophylactic treatment during which hard and soft plaque is removed from the tooth surface and the spaces between the teeth. Surface polishing and fluoridation of the teeth complete the treatment.

Root canal treatment

A root canal treatment becomes necessary when the dental pulp is inflamed or has already died off. The reason in most cases is a far progressed, untreated caries with bacteria having reached the dental pulp. As a consequence the dental pulp is inflamed and dies off.

Root tip resection

The removal of the root tip and inflamed jaw bone tissue. A treatment for teeth that are healthy enough to be preserved: their structure has to be good and they have have a firm hold in the jaw bone.

Root tip resection or cyst removal

A root tip resection or the removal of a cyst (a closed groth containing fluid, mostly benign tumors, eg. In the jaw bone) can lead to a small cavity in the jaw bone. If necessary, it can be filled with bone material.Suction

Suction

Dentures are held in place thanks to the suction effect of the thin film of saliva between the prothesis and the palatine mucosa. The strength of the adhesion depends on the form of the jaw, the oral mucosa and the patient’s salivation. Because of the wider surface a full prothesis usually fits better in the upper jaw than in the lower jaw where we find less convenient anatomical conditions. The tongue-space and the flexiblbe tongue make it difficult to achieve a comparably strong suction.

Tartar

Tartar is a form of hardened dental plaque that cannot be removed neither by rinsing nor by brushing. It is caused by the continual accumulation of minerals from saliva on plaque on the teeth. Tartar does not cause periodontitis, but plaque bacteria can attach to ist rough surface. If there is no plaque on the teeth, tartar cannot build up.

Titanium

It is not long since titanium has been used to manufactur dental inlays, therefore the casting technique hasn’t yet been perfectioned to 100%. Titanium inlays are often faced with ceramic due to its silver grey colour.

Tooth crown

Crowns belong to the permanently fixed dentures. They are used to replace a missing tooth or if the natural crown is severly damaged. Like caps crowns are put on the prepared and ground teeth.

Tooth root

Crowns belong to the permanently fixed dentures. They are used to replace a missing tooth or if the natural crown is severly damaged. Like caps crowns are put on the prepared and ground teeth.

Tooth stump

A ground tooth prepared for a denture.

Ultrasound

With the help of an ultrasound depurator one can gently remove plaque from the tooth necks under the gums. Ultrasound treatment basically makes the plaque explode into very small pieces, after which it falls off.

Veneers

Veneers are thin ceramic layers placed over a tooth surface that are mainly used for cosmetical treatments of the front teeth. Veneers cover discoloured teeth or malpositions.

Wisdom teeth

Of the complete 32 teeth we have, the wisdom teeth only grow between the age of 16 to 18 years, which can cause problems. In some cases though, they fit and integrate well into the set of teeth and do not have to be removed.
